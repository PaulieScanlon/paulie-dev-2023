Your Mastra workflows can now run on Temporal. The new [Temporal integration](https://mastra.ai/guides/deployment/temporal) gives you retries, scheduling, durable state, and execution that survives worker restarts.

Workflow code stays identical. Under the hood, `createWorkflow()` becomes a Temporal workflow and `createStep()` becomes a Temporal activity.

Reach for [Temporal](https://temporal.io) when your workflows call external APIs, stretch over hours or days, or have to outlive a worker restart. You can self-host it or use the hosted version.

Running Mastra workflows on Temporal consists of three parts: create a [Temporal client instance](https://mastra.ai/guides/deployment/temporal#temporal-initialization) that binds Mastra's `createWorkflow` and `createStep` to a Temporal task queue, then create a [worker process](https://mastra.ai/guides/deployment/temporal#running-the-worker) that polls the Temporal task queue and runs your workflows.
